Chapter One

This Is Us

The story of two kids who became best friends

I still remember the day Mom brought you home.

I was supposed to be jealous — the older sibling, dethroned. But the moment I saw your tiny fingers wrap around mine, I knew my job had changed. I wasn't just a kid anymore. I was your protector, your partner-in-crime, your biggest fan.

We fought over the remote. We built forts that collapsed. You ate my share of Maggi more times than I can count. But when the world got loud, you were the only voice that could calm me down.

You taught me patience when you took an hour to tie your shoelaces. You taught me courage when you stood up to that bully twice your size. You taught me that family isn't just blood — it's choosing each other, every single day.

And today, at 25, I don't just see my brother. I see the man I hope to become.

Chapter Four

The Journey of
Becoming You

From the first breath to the man you are today

2001

The Day Everything Changed

You arrived, small and loud, and I held your hand for the very first time. I didn't know it then, but my favorite person had just entered the world.

2007

First Day of School

You cried. I pretended not to. But when I saw you sitting alone at lunch, I sat next to you and shared my tiffin. Some things never change.

2015

The Trouble Years

We fought. A lot. Over clothes, rooms, the TV remote. But when Dad got mad at you, I took the blame. When I fell apart, you built me back up.

2019

Becoming Adults

College, heartbreaks, first jobs. We sat on the terrace at 2 AM more times than I can count — talking about dreams that felt too big to say out loud.

2026

25 & Unstoppable

Look at you now. The world tried to break you, and you smiled back. This is just the beginning, and I can't wait to see what you do next.

Final Chapter

A Letter
For You

25

Dear Brother,

I could write a book about you and still not capture everything you mean to me.

I could tell the world about your kindness, your stubbornness, your ridiculous dance moves, and the way you pretend not to care when you care the most. But some things are just ours.

What I will say is this: you are the best thing that ever happened to me. Not because you're perfect — because you're not. But because you're real. Because you try. Because even on your worst days, you still show up for the people you love.

At 25, you're standing at the edge of everything you've ever wanted. And I need you to know — you deserve it all. The love. The success. The peace. The adventure. Every single good thing that's coming your way.

I hope this year gives you reasons to laugh until your stomach hurts. I hope you find moments so beautiful they take your breath away. I hope you realize, finally, how extraordinary you are.

Happy Birthday. I love you more than words will ever hold.

The Next 25 Years
